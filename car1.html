The Most Luxurious Cars, SUVs, and Trucks You Can Buy in 2024

If you're looking for a car with the most luxurious high-end cabin you can find, these are the ones you want to consider.

By Brian Silvestro and Joey CapparellaUpdated: Jul 23, 2024

Sports cars might be our specialty here at Road & Track, but luxury cars are a close second. Driving a well-built Rolls-Royce or Bentley is a spectacular experience all its own. Sure, these cars may not be built for 0-60 mph times, lap records, or top speed, but they prioritize ride quality, plush interior materials, extravagant features, and street presence above all. Here are the most luxurious cars, SUVs, and pickup trucks you can buy new today, listed from most to least luxurious.

More Car Rankings: Best Luxury Compact SUVs | Best Luxury Hybrid SUVs | Every New Car with a V-8 Engine | Best Cars with Naturally Aspirated Engines

1

2024 Rolls-Royce Phantom

Price: $508,350

Cars simply don't get more opulent than the Rolls-Royce Phantom—the biggest Roller you can buy has always been the ultra-luxury benchmark. With a starting price just under half a million dollars, it better be. There's also an extended-wheelbase version for a bit of extra dough, because space is the ultimate luxury.

Read our full review

2

2025 Rolls-Royce Cullinan

Price: $392,000 (est.)

The Cullinan is the Rolls-Royce of SUVs, so it's no surprise to see it on this list. With a twin-turbo V-12 engine and more features than you could ever imagine, it's the perfect vehicle for driving anywhere in total comfort. And while Rolls-Royces aren't really about performance, there is a Black Badge model with a bit of extra power and a sinister appearance, if you're into that sort of thing.

Check out our first drive

3

2024 Rolls-Royce Ghost

Price: $357,350

Compared to most sedans, the Rolls-Royce Ghost is huge. But on this list, it almost seems like a compact. That means it's small enough to drive yourself, should you want to give your chauffeur the day off. It's still a Rolls-Royce, though, so the back seat is always the best in the house. A twin-turbo V-12 engine is standard, and there are both Black Badge and extended-wheelbase variants if the "base" model isn't enough for you.

Read our full review

4

2024 Rolls-Royce Spectre

Price: $422,750

The Spectre is Rolls-Royce's first-ever production electric car. It's a big, luxury-focused two-door coupe meant to replace the long-dead Phantom Coupe within the company's lineup. With 577 hp and 664 lb-ft of torque on deck, it's no slouch either. When you step out of the rear-hinged front doors, everyone will know you've arrived.

5

2024 Bentley Flying Spur

Price: $217,625

Only in the world of ultra-luxury cars can a quarter-million-dollar sedan look affordable. Don't think for a second, though, that there's anything cheap about the Flying Spur. The new one can hit 207 mph, making it the fastest production sedan on earth. And an even more powerful plug-in-hybrid powertrain is coming to this opulent sedan soon.

This is what we thought when we drove it

6

2025 Bentley Continental GT

Price: $250,000 (est.)

The new Bentley Continental GT packs a big punch, as its plug-in-hybrid drivetrain produces a total of 771 horsepower. This setup perhaps doesn't have the same wow factor as the old W-12, but the Conti remains as over-the-top decadent as ever, with splendid interior materials and modernized styling that communicates its prestige clearly.

More info on the new PHEV Conti GT

7

2024 Bentley Bentayga

Price: $205,925

All you really need to know regarding the Bentayga SUV's luxury credentials is that it offers a $160,000 dashboard clock as an option. It's a Breitling tourbillon, the only such clock to be fitted to a car. Otherwise, the Bentayga is a Bentley through and through, and it offers both twin-turbo V-8 and V-6 plug-in-hybrid engine choices.

Here's why we love it

8

2024 Mercedes-Maybach S-Class

Price: $199,450

If you want the ultimate expression of luxury from Mercedes-Benz, look for the Maybach badge. The latest top-level S-Class is coated in luxury, with a world-class interior and a classic two-tone exterior with plenty of chrome. A twin-turbo V-8 is standard in the S 580, but if you care about luxury, won't you want to opt for the S 680 and its silky V-12? It's only a $36,000 price premium, after all.

We're partial to the V-12 model

9

2024 Mercedes-Maybach GLS-Class

Price: $175,500

The GLS 600 is Mercedes's first Maybach-branded SUV (there's now also the electric Maybach EQS SUV, and it certainly lives up to the name. With ostentatious looks and a massive rear seating area, it has everything you need to cruise the outback in perfect comfort. Just don't expect to see a third row of seats, as the Maybach is strictly a four-seater due to its special two-place rear-seat setup that includes extendable footrests and all manner of niceties.

Here are all the specs

10

2025 BMW 7-Series / i7

View Photos

Price: $97,575

If you can get past those comically oversized kidney grilles and weird split headlights, the 7-Series is a lovely piece of machinery that does the luxury thing extremely well. Though, like many cars on this list, you should keep in mind the heavy depreciation hit you'll take if you buy it new. There's also the electric i7, which looks identical to the gas-powered model but offers a high-powered M70 variant with 650 horsepower.

Read our full review

11

2025 BMW Alpina XB7

Price: $153,395

The BMW X7 is like an SUV version of the 7-Series. That means all the features of BMW's flagship sedan, plus the added perks of a high seating position and tons of cabin space. The Alpina version takes it one step further, with bespoke touches and even better materials. Oh, and it comes with a 630-horsepower V-8 engine.

Here's our full first drive review

12

2025 Audi A8 / S8

Price: $93,295

The A8 is actually one of the more affordable cars on this list, but you wouldn't know it by sitting inside. Plenty of standard features and an optional rear infotainment system (with its own removable tablet) will make any tech-savvy passenger happy. The standard model comes with a turbocharged V-6, but there's also the S8 with a powerful twin-turbo V-8.

Read our full review of the high-performance S8

13

2025 Cadillac Escalade

Price: $85,000 (est.)

At this point, the Escalade name carries almost as much weight as the Cadillac brand. This full-sizer has defined what it means to be a luxury SUV for some time now, and Cadillac is expanding the lineup further with the electric Escalade IQ. It hasn't forgotten about the standard gas model, though, which gets numerous upgrades for 2025 including a massive dashboard-spanning screen.

See the New Model's 55-Inch Screen

14

2024 Lexus LS

Price: $80,685

The LS is Lexus's top-tier sedan, with features such 28-way power seats, an immensely quiet interior, and a twin-turbo V-6 engine with enough grunt to rocket the car to 60 mph in just 4.5 seconds. There's also a hybrid model available, and it offers a hands-free driver-assistance system.

Read about the Million Mile Lexus LS400

15

2024 Lincoln Navigator

Price: $85,260

Like the Escalade, the Navigator was an early entry in the full-size luxury SUV segment, and it's evolved into quite the boulevard cruiser. The exterior styling is bold and brash, but the interior is plush and subdued, and the Black Label models offer a range of interior color schemes that each have a different vibe. The extended-wheelbase Navigator L offers a bit more space for cargo and third-row passengers.

Read our full review

16

2024 Genesis G90

Price: $90,450

The Genesis G90 is priced competitively for a flagship luxury sedan, but don't think that this makes it a bargain-basement alternative to Mercedes and BMW. Instead, it punches above its weight thanks to its upscale design inside and out and generous helping of features including available massaging seats, power-closing doors, and a fingerprint scanner.

Check out our first drive right here

17

2025 Ram 1500 Tungsten

Price: $89,070

Bet you weren't expecting to see a truck on this list, were you? Well, demand for luxurious pickups has skyrocketed in America, which led Ram to create a new trim level for the 2025 Ram 1500 called Tungsten. It's the top-of-the-line Ram in every sense, with features including a power tailgate, 24-way power front seats with massage, special interior materials, and a 23-speaker Klipsch audio system.
